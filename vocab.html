• Ethos:(appeal to ethics) is a way of convincing an audience with the credibility of the persuader. Can be a notable or experienced figure or a celebrity.
  • Pathos:(appeal to emotions) convincing the audience by creating an emotional response to an impassioned plea or convincing story.
  • Logos:(appeal to logic) is a way of persuading the audience with reason, using facts and figures.
  • Allusion:An expression designed to call to mind without mentioning it explicitly
  • Analogy:A comparison between two things; typically for the purpose of explanation or classification
  • Allegory:a story, poem, or picture that can be interpreted to reveal a hidden meaning, typically a moral or political one.
  • Paradox: A statement that contradicts itself or that must be both true and untrue at the same time
  • Ambiguities:Confusing the reader and hinder the meaning of the text. Can have humor added to the text.
  • Anecdotes:A short story that is significant to the topic at hand; usually adding personal knowledge or experience.
  • Symbolism:Any image that stands for something else. It could be as simple as a letter, which is a symbol for a sound
  • Irony:When there are two contradictory meanings of the same situation, event, image, sentence or phrase. Difference between expectation and reality.
  • Diction:Word choice & phrasing in any written or spoken text.
  • Parallelism:Elements in sentences that are grammatically similar or identical in structure, sound, meaning or meter.
  • Rhetorical Questions:Used to persuade or subtly influence the audience. It’s a question asked for a effect, not an answer.
  • Anaphora:Repeating a sequence of words at the beginning of neighboring clauses; leading emphasis.
  • Alliteration:A repetition of identical initial consonant sounds in closely associated syllables.
  • Tone:The ‘feel’ of a piece of writing. There's a ‘style’ or ‘voice’ in writing
  • Repetition:Is the repeating of a word or phrase. Used in poetry and throughout all genres
  • Imagery:A pattern that aims to make vivid a place, object, character or group
  • Organization:The internal structure of a piece like compare & contrast, cause & effect and chronological order
